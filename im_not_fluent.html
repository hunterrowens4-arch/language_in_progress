Language in Progress

I'm Not Fluent - I'm Just Logging What Clicks

January 29, 2026

I didn't always like foreign languages. I took Spanish in high school and just didn't care. Years later I wanted to learn it again, but by that pointmost of what I learned was gone. I had to start over.


Eventually I started getting frustrated because I felt like I wasn't making progress, so I added Japanese for variety, then Russian. I'm self-taught, experimenting with different strategies and resources to keep my learning moving.


Learning stays interesting that way - but it doesn't make it easy. Every language has its own challenges, but a lot of the struggles are the same across languages: grammar rules or knowing what a word means, but not when to use it. And is it just me or are numbers hard in every language for some reason?


Since I've started learning languages seriously I've made it a goal to learn multiple. So I'm always looking for new ways to study better. That's why I started writing this. As I find new resources that work and as difficult concepts click, I'll add them here and explain how I got there. This log is a growing resource for myself - and any other language learners who happen to find it.


There logs may be messy. My opinions may change. I'm not fluent, and I will be wrong sometimes.


But if you're learning a language too, you're welcome to learn with me.
